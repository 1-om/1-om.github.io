# Can Machines Think?
## A Scientific Resolution of the Lovelace-Turing Debate

This paper argues that the Lovelace-Turing debate becomes scientifically tractable once "origination" is treated as a multi-dimensional profile of novelty, value, causal independence, and adaptive revision rather than as creation from nowhere.

The core claim is simple: machines will produce correct proofs, boring proofs, and wrong proofs. That does not collapse research into proof checking. The scarce skill moves upstream to problem selection, definition choice, explanatory framing, and judgment.

## Why this matters

- Verification is easier than search, but research is not just search.
- AI will lower the cost of generating candidate proofs and arguments.
- The human task becomes deciding what is worth proving and why it matters.
- That judgment is the research skill the paper defends.
